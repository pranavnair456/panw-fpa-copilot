# FY2026Q3 Executive Brief

**Headline:** Total revenue of $3,002M came in $59M above the guidance midpoint (+2.0%, favorable), but the quality of the beat warrants attention: nearly all of the outperformance versus our model was inorganic.

**The Forecast Read.** Against management's guidance midpoint of $2,943M, the quarter cleared by $59M (+2.0%). Measured against our own model, organic revenue of $2,614M beat our $2,564M forecast by $50M. That organic beat is real, but it is the smaller story — inorganic contribution from the CyberArk and Chronosphere acquisitions accounted for 89% of the total beat versus our forecast.

**The Variance Story (organic vs. inorganic).** The bridge is clean: starting from our organic forecast of $2,564M, the business delivered $50M of organic outperformance to reach $2,614M, then layered on $388M of inorganic revenue from CyberArk and Chronosphere to land at the $3,002M reported total. The takeaway for the CFO: the headline number is flattered by M&A. Underlying organic momentum is positive but modest at $50M above plan.

**The Driver / Signal Read.** The forward indicators tell the same dual story. RPO (backlog) rose $2,400M to $18,400M (+15%), but $1,800M of that was inorganic versus only $600M organic. NGS ARR grew $1,800M to $8,100M (+29%), again concentrated in the acquisitions ($1,600M inorganic, $200M organic). The acquisitions are materially expanding the backlog and ARR base; organic contribution to both metrics is comparatively thin. Management's transcript tone was positive, confident, and explicitly raising guidance.

**What to Watch.** Q4 organic revenue is forecast at $2,805M, within an 80% band of $2,736M–$2,875M. The central question for next quarter is whether organic growth re-accelerates toward that forecast or whether reported strength remains dependent on the acquired bases. Watch the organic split within RPO and NGS ARR as the leading tell on durable, non-M&A momentum.
